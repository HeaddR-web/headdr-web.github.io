The Coziest Throw Blankets, Ranked

Updated 15 June 2026 · 6 min read · Cozylore

If we could recommend one thing to make a room cozy, it's a throw blanket. Draped over a sofa or the foot of a bed, it adds warmth, texture and that "come sit down" feeling instantly. But not all throws are equal. Here's how to pick the coziest one — ranked by how snug each type actually feels.

The cozy ranking, by material

1. Chunky knit (the cozy king)

Big, chunky knits win on pure cozy factor. The deep weave casts soft shadows and looks incredible draped. Go for wool or a soft acrylic blend. This is the one that shows up in every cozy photo.

2. Faux fur / sherpa (the warmest)

For pure warmth and softness against the skin, faux fur or sherpa-lined throws are unbeatable in winter.

3. Waffle / cotton (the year-round pick)

Breathable and not too hot, waffle-weave cotton throws work in every season — cozy without overheating in spring and autumn.

4. Wool (the classic)

Naturally warm, durable and elegant. A wool throw lasts years and only looks better with age.

What to look for

  • Size: at least 130×170 cm so you can actually wrap up, not just drape.
  • Weight: heavier knits feel more luxurious; lighter waffle weaves are more versatile.
  • Color: warm neutrals (oatmeal, rust, cream, sage) photograph best and match any room.
  • Care: check if it's machine washable — chunky knits often aren't.

The short answer

For looks, get a chunky knit in a warm neutral. For pure warmth, get a faux fur. Many cozy homes keep one of each — one to look at, one to actually burrow under.
