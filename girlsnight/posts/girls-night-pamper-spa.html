The At-Home Pamper & Spa Night, Step by Step

Updated 15 June 2026 · 5 min read · Girls Night HQ

A pamper night is the easiest girls night to host — it's low-energy, everyone leaves feeling amazing, and the "decorations" are just candles and soft lighting. Here's how to turn your living room into a spa for one evening, step by step.

Set the scene

  • Dim everything. Lamps and candles only — overhead lights kill the mood instantly.
  • Soft playlist. Spa or lo-fi, low volume.
  • Cosy textures. Robes, blankets, fluffy socks — tell everyone to come comfy.
  • A drinks moment. Herbal tea, infused water and a glass of something bubbly (or a mocktail).

The pamper-night running order

A loose order keeps it flowing without feeling scheduled:

  • 1. Cleanse & mask. Everyone washes their face and applies a mask together (peak photo moment).
  • 2. Hands & feet. While masks set, do a mini mani-pedi — soak, file, paint.
  • 3. Hair treatments. A hair mask or scalp massage round.
  • 4. Rinse & moisturise. Off come the masks, on goes the good moisturiser.
  • 5. Wind down. Tea, a feel-good film and chats. Pamper nights end soft.

Nice-to-have extras

  • Cucumber or eye masks for the full cliché (in the best way).
  • A foot spa or warm basin with epsom salts.
  • A gua sha or facial roller to share around.
  • A little gift bag — a mask and a mini polish each to take home.

Keep it inclusive

Have a fragrance-free and a sensitive-skin option, mention ingredients for anyone with allergies, and never push a treatment on someone who'd rather just sip tea and watch. The whole point is everyone relaxing.
