21 Girls Night Games That Actually Get Everyone Laughing

Updated 15 June 2026 · 6 min read · Girls Night HQ

A great girls night doesn't need a plan — it needs one good game to break the ice, and the rest takes care of itself. Below are 21 games sorted by how much setup they need, so you can grab a quick one the moment the energy dips, or commit to a full chaotic round when everyone's settled in.

No-prop games (start here)

These need nothing but people. Perfect for the first 20 minutes while everyone arrives.

  • Never Have I Ever — the classic. Hold up five fingers, take one down for each thing you've done.
  • Most Likely To — "Who's most likely to text their ex at 2am?" Everyone points. Loudest reaction wins.
  • Two Truths and a Lie — surprisingly revealing once the wine kicks in.
  • The Hot Seat — one person answers rapid-fire questions from everyone else for 60 seconds.
  • Celebrity — guess the famous name on your forehead from yes/no questions.
  • Would You Rather — keep them spicy or silly, your call.
  • The Story Game — one word each, around the circle, building one ridiculous story.

Phone games (zero printing, zero shopping)

  • Heads Up! — the charades-style app; hold the phone to your forehead and guess.
  • Psych! — make up fake answers to real trivia and fool your friends.
  • Camera roll roulette — random person scrolls to a photo and explains it. Always a story.
  • The playlist game — everyone adds one song; guess who added what.

Card & box games worth owning

If girls night is a regular thing, a couple of reusable games earn their keep fast:

  • A "what do you meme" style game — caption cards, instant laughs.
  • A truth-or-dare card deck — takes the pressure off thinking them up.
  • A trivia or "how well do you know each other" deck — great for close friends.
  • A drawing/guessing game — chaotic and very photo-worthy.

Drinking-game versions (optional)

Any game above becomes a drinking game with one rule: lose a round, take a sip. Keep water on the table, make sure there are zero-proof options, and never pressure anyone — the point is laughing together, not who finishes first. (See our drinks guide for easy mocktails so everyone's included.)

  • Ring of Fire / Kings — the all-in-one card drinking game.
  • Beer Pong — works with any drink, including alcohol-free.
  • "Waterfall" Never Have I Ever — sip instead of lowering fingers.

How to actually run the games

  • Start easy. No-prop games first — nobody wants rules before they've taken their coat off.
  • One host, loosely. Someone needs to say "okay, next game" or it fizzles.
  • Keep rounds short. End a game while it's still fun, not when it's died.
  • Have a prize. Even a silly one — a face mask, a mini bottle of something — raises the stakes.
