Girls Night In Ideas on a Budget: 20 Cheap, Fun Things to Do

Updated 16 June 2026 · 7 min read · Girls Night HQ

The best girls nights are almost never the expensive ones. A restaurant bill split six ways, a pricey night out, the taxi home — none of that is where the laughing happens. The laughing happens on the sofa, in pyjamas, with a snack table and a game nobody can stop playing. Here are 20 ways to host a brilliant girls night in for next to nothing, plus the handful of small buys that are genuinely worth it.

The golden rule: make it potluck

One person hosting and paying for everything is how budgets (and friendships) get strained. Split it instead: the host provides the space and the playlist, and everyone brings one thing — a bottle, a snack, a dessert, a game. Suddenly a full spread costs each person almost nothing, and nobody's stuck in the kitchen all night. Set it up in your group chat with a quick "who's bringing what" list so you don't end up with five bags of crisps and no drinks.

Free (or nearly free) activities

These cost nothing but somehow make the best memories:

  • A no-prop game marathon — Never Have I Ever, Most Likely To, Two Truths and a Lie. See our full girls night games guide for 20+ that need zero shopping.
  • Themed movie marathon — pick a trilogy, a decade or a "so bad it's good" night. Our movie night setup shows how to make the sofa feel like a cinema.
  • DIY spa night — face masks, painted nails, hair masks from the kitchen. The whole ritual is in our pamper & spa guide.
  • Bake-off or recipe swap — everyone makes one thing; you eat all of it. Obviously.
  • Closet swap — everyone brings clothes they no longer wear and goes home with "new" ones.
  • Throwback night — old photos, your teenage playlist, the music videos you knew by heart.

Snacks that look generous but cost little

You don't need a catered grazing board to impress. A few cheap moves go a long way:

  • Popcorn three ways — one big bag, three bowls, three toppings (sweet, salty, spicy).
  • A "use what you have" board — crackers, whatever cheese is on offer, grapes, nuts, olives, a pot of jam. Pile it high; abundance reads as effort.
  • Dips and dippers — hummus, salsa, guac with veg sticks and pitta. Cheap and always disappears.
  • One "centrepiece" bake — a tray of brownies or cookies makes the whole table feel special for a couple of euros.

Drinks without the bar bill

  • One signature jug — a big batch cocktail or punch stretches cheap ingredients further than individual drinks and means no bartending all night.
  • Mocktails for everyone — sparkling water, juice and frozen fruit looks festive and costs pennies. Recipes are in our cocktails & mocktails guide.
  • BYOB — everyone brings their own favourite; the host doesn't foot the drinks bill.

Decorate with what you already own

You almost certainly already have what you need. Pull every blanket and cushion into one cosy pile. Light the candles you've been "saving". Dim the main lights and add the string lights. Use your nicest glasses — even mismatched ones look intentional by candlelight. If you want a little extra, a few balloons or a cheap paper garland in one colour pulls a table together instantly (more easy looks in our theme & decoration guide).

The few buys actually worth it

Budget doesn't mean buy nothing — it means buy things you'll reuse for every future night in. The short list:

  • Warm-white string lights — the atmosphere upgrade above.
  • One good party card game — a one-time buy that powers dozens of nights.
  • A reusable popcorn bowl set — because the popcorn is non-negotiable.

A budget girls night, start to finish

  • Plan: set the potluck list in the group chat, pick a loose theme or movie.
  • Prep: tidy one room, pile the blankets, set out bowls, string the lights.
  • Arrive: everyone drops their dish and drink, host hands out the first round.
  • Flow: snacks and a game first, movie or spa later, photos throughout.

Total spend if you split it? Often less than a single cocktail out — and a far better night.
