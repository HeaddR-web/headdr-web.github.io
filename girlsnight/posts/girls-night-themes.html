12 Girls Night Theme & Decoration Ideas

Updated 15 June 2026 · 6 min read · Girls Night HQ

Picking a theme is the single fastest way to make a girls night feel planned instead of thrown together — it decides your colours, your drinks, even the dress code, so every other choice gets easier. Here are 12 themes plus the few decorations that actually move the needle.

12 girls night themes to steal

  • Hot pink & disco — balloons, a disco ball, sequins. The classic glam night.
  • Galentine's — pink and red, hearts, lots of candles and chocolate.
  • Cocktails & couture — everyone dresses up, signature cocktails, jazz playlist.
  • Spa & self-care — robes, face masks, calm lighting (see the pamper guide).
  • Movie marathon — a genre or a trilogy, blankets and a popcorn bar.
  • Wine & paint — canvases, a how-to video, zero artistic pressure.
  • 2000s throwback — Y2K outfits, a nostalgia playlist, butterfly clips.
  • Tropical / tiki — bright colours, fruity drinks, leis and umbrellas.
  • Cosy autumn night in — warm tones, candles, comfort food.
  • Game night — board games and party packs as the main event.
  • Bake-off — everyone makes something, then you judge.
  • Bridal / hen night in — sash, photo wall, a round of party games.

The decorations that actually matter

Skip the clutter. Three things do 90% of the work:

  • A focal backdrop — balloon arch, fringe curtain or a fabric drape. This is your photo wall.
  • Lighting — fairy lights, LED strips or a disco ball completely change the mood after dark.
  • A styled table — a runner, candles, your drinks and the grazing board, all in your theme colours.

Set the scene in 30 minutes

  • Pick the colours from your theme and buy decorations in just those two or three shades.
  • Build the backdrop first — everything else arranges around it.
  • Make a photo corner — props, good light, a fun sign. People will use it all night.
  • One playlist. Match it to the theme and let it run.
