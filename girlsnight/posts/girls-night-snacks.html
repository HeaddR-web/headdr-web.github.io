Easy Girls Night Snacks & a Show-Stopping Grazing Board

Updated 15 June 2026 · 5 min read · Girls Night HQ

The best girls night food is the kind you can put out once and forget about — no plating, no hot dishes, no leaving the room every ten minutes. That's why a grazing board is the move: it looks impressive, it's basically assembly, and it keeps people grazing all night.

How to build a grazing board (the formula)

Work in this order and a board comes together in 15 minutes:

  • 1. Anchors. 3–4 cheeses (a soft, a hard, a blue or flavoured) and 2–3 cured meats.
  • 2. Carbs. Crackers, breadsticks, sliced baguette — fan them out in groups.
  • 3. Something fresh. Grapes, figs, strawberries, cherry tomatoes.
  • 4. Something dippy. Hummus, a chutney, honey for the cheese.
  • 5. Salty crunch. Olives, nuts, pretzels.
  • 6. Fill every gap. Dried fruit and chocolate squares tucked into the spaces — no empty board.

Rough guide: as a main, plan ~150–200g per person across the board; as a nibble alongside dinner, half that.

Sweet snacks that look fancy (but aren't)

  • Chocolate-dipped strawberries — melt chocolate, dip, chill. Ten minutes, big impact.
  • Macarons or mini pastries — shop-bought, arranged on a cake stand.
  • A "dessert board" — brownies, cookies, marshmallows and fruit with a chocolate dip.
  • Popcorn two ways — one sweet, one salty, in matching bowls.

Warm bites if you want one hot thing

  • Loaded nachos — one tray, everyone digs in.
  • Mini pizzas — toppings out, let people build their own.
  • Garlic-bread bites or spring rolls — oven to table, done.

Make-ahead so you can actually enjoy the night

  • Board: assemble dry parts ahead, add fresh fruit and crackers just before guests arrive.
  • Dips: make a day before — they're better for it.
  • Sweet things: dipped fruit and dessert boards can be chilled hours ahead.
  • Allergies: ask in the group chat first and label anything with nuts.
