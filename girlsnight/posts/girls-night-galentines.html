Galentine's Day Party Ideas: How to Host the Best Galentine's

Updated 16 June 2026 · 7 min read · Girls Night HQ

Valentine's Day gets all the attention — but the day before it belongs to your friends. Galentine's Day is the excuse to gather your favourite women, pour something pink and spend an evening (or a brunch) celebrating the friendships that actually carry you through the year. No partner required, no pressure, just good people and a great spread. Here's everything you need to host one your group will want to repeat every February.

What is Galentine's Day — and when is it?

Galentine's Day falls on 13 February, the day before Valentine's. It started as a joke on the TV show Parks and Recreation — Leslie Knope's "ladies celebrating ladies" brunch — and it stuck, because the idea is genuinely lovely: a whole day dedicated to your friendships. If the 13th lands on an awkward weekday, don't overthink it. Most groups just move the party to the nearest weekend and call it close enough.

Pick a format: brunch or evening?

Galentine's works two ways, and the format decides the whole vibe:

  • Brunch (the classic). Mimosas, pastries, a fruit platter and waffles. Bright, daytime, very Leslie Knope. Great if your group has kids or early mornings the next day.
  • Evening in. Cocktails, a grazing board, candles and a movie or a game. More glam, more relaxed, easier to stretch into a long, lingering night.

Either way, the recipe is the same: a loose theme, good food, a signature drink, one fun activity and a small favour to send everyone home with. Let's build it.

Choose a theme (it makes everything easier)

A theme isn't fussy — it just gives you a colour palette and a shopping shortcut so the decor, the table and even the drinks all match. The most popular Galentine's themes:

  • Pink & red everything — the default, and for good reason. Hearts, roses, blush and crimson. Impossible to get wrong.
  • "Galentine's brunch" — soft pastels, fresh flowers, gold accents, lots of natural light.
  • Disco love — hot pink, sequins, a disco ball and a sparkly playlist.
  • Anti-Valentine's — black, dark red and a bit of tongue-in-cheek humour for the gloriously single.
  • Galentine's spa day — robes, face masks and calm lighting (steal the whole ritual from our pamper & spa night guide).

The food: keep it pretty and make-ahead

Galentine's food should look generous and take almost no effort on the day. The move is a grazing board in your theme colours — strawberries, raspberries, pink macarons, heart-shaped cookies, brie, dark chocolate and a little pot of jam. For brunch, add mini pastries, a fruit platter and something warm like waffles or french toast.

Easy themed touches

  • Heart-shaped cookie cutters on toast, sandwiches, brownies — instant theme, zero skill.
  • Pink dips: beetroot hummus or a raspberry yoghurt dip for the fruit.
  • A "build your own" station — mimosa bar, doughnut wall, or topping-your-own cupcakes.

The drinks: one signature, plus a zero-proof option

Pick one signature drink so you're not playing bartender all night. A pink gin spritz, a raspberry French 75, or the brunch staple — a mimosa bar with a few juices to mix. Always set out a matching mocktail so everyone's included; a sparkling raspberry lemonade in the same glasses looks just as festive. Activities: the bit that makes it a party

You don't need many — one or two is plenty:

  • A friendship-themed game. "How well do you know each other", or any of our girls night games — they're perfect for a warm, talkative group.
  • A craft. Galentine's card-making, friendship bracelets, or decorating mini bouquets to take home. Low-key and very photogenic.
  • Compliment circle. Cheesy, yes — but going around the table saying one thing you love about each person is the moment everyone secretly remembers.
  • A photo corner. Prop the balloon garland against a wall, add a ring light or good lamp, and let the camera roll fill itself.

Gift bags & party favours

The little send-home bag is what turns a hangout into a Galentine's. It doesn't need to be expensive — it needs to be thoughtful and on-theme. Easy fillers that always land:

  • A sheet face mask or a mini nail polish
  • A few good chocolates or pink-wrapped sweets
  • A scented mini candle or a bath bomb
  • A handwritten note — genuinely the cheapest and most-loved item in the bag

A simple Galentine's timeline

  • 1 week before: send the invite, pick the theme, order decor and favour bits.
  • Day before: assemble gift bags, prep anything make-ahead, chill the drinks.
  • 2 hours before: blow up the balloon garland, set the table, build the grazing board.
  • On arrival: hand everyone the signature drink, start an easy game, let it flow.
