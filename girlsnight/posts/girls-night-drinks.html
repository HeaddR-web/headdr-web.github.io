Girls Night Cocktails & Mocktails Everyone Will Love

Updated 15 June 2026 · 6 min read · Girls Night HQ

The secret to drinks at a girls night isn't a cocktail menu — it's one big-batch signature drink you make ahead, plus a couple of easy options so nobody's stuck at the kitchen counter shaking drinks all evening. Here's how to do it the lazy-but-impressive way.

Pick one signature drink (and batch it)

Choose a single cocktail, multiply the recipe by your headcount, and mix it in a jug or drink dispenser before anyone arrives. Add the fizzy or fresh parts last so it stays lively. Reliable batch cocktails:

  • Aperol Spritz — 3 parts prosecco, 2 parts Aperol, 1 part soda. Effortless and pretty.
  • Pink gin spritz — gin, lemonade or tonic, a splash of cranberry, lots of ice.
  • Frozen margarita — blend tequila, lime, triple sec and ice; rim the glasses with salt.
  • Sangria — wine, chopped fruit, a little brandy; make it hours ahead so it infuses.
  • Espresso martini — the late-night classic if you have a shaker and a coffee maker.

Don't forget the mocktails

Someone's always driving, pregnant, doing a dry month or just not drinking — and a good alcohol-free option means they're not stuck with a glass of water. Make the mocktail look as good as the cocktail:

  • Virgin mojito — lime, mint, sugar, soda. Fresh and zero effort.
  • Cranberry & ginger fizz — cranberry juice, ginger ale, a squeeze of lime.
  • Faux-jito spritz — alcohol-free aperitif + soda for the grown-up bitter note.
  • Sparkling fruit punch — a big batch everyone can share.

The glassware makes it

You don't need a full bar, but matching glasses instantly make drinks feel like an event. Coupe glasses for anything chic, hi-balls for spritzes, and a drinks dispenser for self-serve batches so you're not playing bartender all night.

Quick hosting tips

  • More ice than you think. It runs out first, every time.
  • Garnish station. A board of lime wedges, berries and mint lets people customise.
  • Water on the table. A jug with fruit looks intentional and keeps everyone good.
  • Pace it with food. See the snacks & grazing board guide.
